Frequently Asked Questions About Sublimation Printing

What is sublimation printing?

Sublimation printing is a digital printing technique that uses heat to transfer dye onto materials such as fabric, plastic, or coated metal. Unlike traditional printing methods that apply ink on top of a surface, sublimation turns solid dye into gas that bonds directly with the fibres of polyester-based materials. The result is a permanent, vibrant, and crack-resistant print that becomes part of the material itself.

How does sublimation printing work?

The process involves three key steps:

  1. Design creation: Your artwork is prepared digitally, allowing for unlimited colours and photographic detail.
  2. Heat transfer: The design is printed onto special transfer paper using sublimation inks, then placed on the target material.
  3. Pressing: A heat press applies high temperature (around 200 °C) and pressure, causing the ink to turn into gas and permanently bond with the polyester fibres.

Because the dye penetrates the material rather than sitting on top, the finished print is smooth to the touch and incredibly durable.

Is sublimation printing better than screen printing?

It depends on your project. Here is a quick comparison:

  • Sublimation produces full-colour, photographic-quality prints with no texture on the fabric. It is ideal for detailed designs, gradients, and small to medium batches. No minimum order is required.
  • Screen printing is more cost-effective for large runs of simple designs with few colours. Each colour requires a separate screen, which adds setup cost and time.

For branded corporate apparel in Eswatini, sublimation is often the preferred choice because of its durability, colour accuracy, and professional finish.

What materials can be sublimated?

Sublimation works best on polyester or polyester-coated surfaces. Common items include:

  • T-shirts and polo shirts (polyester or poly-blend)
  • Custom mugs and coasters
  • Keyholders and phone covers
  • Metal name badges and signage
  • Tote bags and lanyards

Important: Cotton fabrics do not absorb sublimation dye effectively, so they are not suitable for this method. If you need cotton garments, screen printing or heat-transfer vinyl may be better options.

How long does sublimation printing last?

Sublimation prints are permanent. Because the dye becomes part of the fabric rather than sitting on top of it, the print will not crack, peel, or fade under normal washing conditions. Properly cared-for sublimated garments can last for years without any visible deterioration in print quality. This makes sublimation the go-to choice for corporate uniforms, sports kits, and promotional merchandise that needs to look great wash after wash.

Can sublimation print white?

No — sublimation ink is transparent, so it cannot print white on dark fabrics. The fabric colour shows through the ink. For white designs on dark garments, alternatives like screen printing or heat-transfer vinyl are recommended. Sublimation works best on white or light-coloured polyester items where the fabric colour does not interfere with the design.

Yes. One of the biggest advantages of sublimation is the ability to print edge-to-edge, covering the entire garment with your design. This is known as all-over sublimation and is popular for event T-shirts, sports jerseys, and promotional campaigns that demand bold, eye-catching visuals. When Should You Choose Sublimation Printing?

Sublimation is the right choice when you need:

  • Full-colour designs with gradients, photographs, or complex artwork
  • Durable prints that will not crack, peel, or fade over time
  • Small batch orders without expensive setup costs
  • All-over prints that cover the entire garment
  • Professional corporate apparel that looks sharp wash after wash

If your project involves cotton fabrics, dark garments with white elements, or very large runs of simple one- or two-colour designs, other methods like screen printing or vinyl may be more suitable. The team at Sibeve can advise you on the best method for your specific needs.
