Frequently Asked Questions About Promotional Products

What are the best promotional products for small businesses?

The best promotional products for small businesses are items that offer high daily visibility at a low cost per impression. Top choices include:

  • Branded pens — affordable, universally useful, and passed between people
  • Tote bags — walking billboards that get seen everywhere
  • T-shirts and caps — high visibility, especially at events and in public spaces
  • Custom mugs — used daily in offices and homes, keeping your brand front and centre
  • Notebooks and diaries — practical corporate gifts that last all year

For businesses in Eswatini, branded apparel and custom drinkware consistently deliver the strongest return on investment because of the hot climate and outdoor culture.

How much should I budget for promotional products?

Most businesses allocate 5 to 10 percent of their overall marketing budget to promotional products. But rather than focusing on unit price alone, consider your cost per impression. A branded T-shirt worn hundreds of times may cost far less per impression than a cheaper item that gets discarded after a day. Sibeve offers options for every budget — from affordable branded pens and keyholders to premium corporate gift sets and executive hampers.

How do I choose the right promotional product for my brand?

Start by asking three questions:

  1. Will the recipient actually use this? Practical items that solve a real need get kept longer.
  2. Does it align with our brand values? A tech company might choose USB drives or power banks; a wellness brand might prefer water bottles or fitness gear.
  3. Will it be seen by others? Items worn or carried in public — like caps, bags, and shirts — generate far more impressions than items kept in a drawer.

The best promotional products are the ones your audience reaches for every day. When an item becomes part of someone's routine, your brand becomes part of their life.

What is the ROI on promotional products?

Studies consistently show that promotional products deliver a lower cost per impression than almost any other advertising medium. Key statistics:

  • 85 percent of people who receive a promotional item can recall the advertiser
  • The average kept item generates over 4,000 impressions over its lifetime
  • 79 percent of consumers say they are more likely to do business with a company they have received a promotional product from

The key to maximising ROI is choosing items people actually want to keep and use. A cheap item that gets thrown away has zero ROI. A well-chosen item that stays in daily use for a year is marketing gold.

How long does it take to produce promotional products?

Production time varies by product and printing method:

  • Sublimation and digital printing: 3 to 7 working days
  • Screen printing: 5 to 10 working days depending on order size
  • Embroidery: 5 to 12 working days
  • Custom corporate gifts and hampers: 7 to 14 working days

At Sibeve, we offer rush services for urgent deadlines and always communicate clear timelines upfront. Planning ahead ensures you get the best pricing and avoids last-minute rush fees.

What is the minimum order quantity for promotional products?

Minimum order quantities vary by product and method. Sublimation printing has no minimum order — you can order as few as one item. Screen printing typically requires a minimum of 20 to 50 units to be cost-effective. At Sibeve, we accommodate both small and large orders, so whether you need 10 branded shirts for a startup team or 5,000 items for a national campaign, we can help.

Should I use the same promotional product for all events?

No — different events and audiences call for different items. A one-size-fits-all approach wastes budget and misses opportunities. Consider these scenarios:

  • Trade shows and exhibitions: Lightweight, eye-catching items like branded bags, lanyards, and pens that attendees will carry around the venue
  • Corporate gifting: Premium items like branded notebooks, executive pens, or gift sets that convey quality and appreciation
  • Outdoor events and sports: Caps, cooler bags, water bottles, and branded apparel that suit the environment
  • Employee onboarding: Welcome kits with branded stationery, mugs, and apparel that build team identity from day one

How do I make my promotional products stand out?

In a world awash with branded merchandise, differentiation matters. Here are proven strategies:

  • Invest in design quality — a well-designed item reflects well on your brand; a cheap-looking one does the opposite
  • Choose premium materials — people keep high-quality items and discard flimsy ones
  • Add functional value — a bag with extra pockets, a pen with a stylus, a notebook with a planner section
  • Use bold, consistent branding — your logo and colours should be instantly recognisable
  • Personalise when possible — adding a recipient's name transforms a generic item into a keepsake

Promotional Products by Category: What Works Best

At Sibeve, we produce a wide range of promotional items. Here is a quick guide to the most popular categories and when to use them:

Branded Apparel

T-shirts, polo shirts, caps, and jackets are the most visible promotional products available. Every time someone wears your branded apparel in public, they advertise your business to everyone they pass. Sublimation printing on polyester garments delivers the most vibrant, long-lasting results.

Corporate Gifts

Executive pens, leather-bound notebooks, branded diaries, and premium gift sets are ideal for client appreciation, end-of-year gifting, and VIP events. These items convey quality and thoughtfulness, strengthening business relationships.

Event and Exhibition Items

Pull-up banners, branded gazebos, table covers, and exhibition stands transform your event presence from forgettable to unforgettable. These items create a cohesive, professional look that attracts visitors and builds credibility.

Everyday Promotional Items

Mugs, pens, keyholders, lanyards, and tote bags are affordable, practical, and universally appreciated. They are the workhorses of promotional marketing — low cost, high impressions, and broad appeal.
